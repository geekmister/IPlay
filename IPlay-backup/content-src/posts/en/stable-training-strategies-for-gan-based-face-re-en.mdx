# Stable Training Strategies for GAN-based Face Restoration

> Perspective: Identity constraints are crucial to prevent identity drift in restored faces.

## Research Question

This article focuses on GAN face restoration: improving interpretability, stability, and deployability while preserving strong performance.

## Method Perspective

1. Define task constraints before increasing model complexity.
2. Use both perceptual and objective metrics for evaluation.
3. Replay failure cases during training to reduce tail-risk.

## Evaluation Suggestions

- Report not only peak scores, but also variance and worst-case behavior.
- Add cross-domain validation to avoid single-dataset overfitting.
- Include latency and memory costs for engineering decisions.

## Representative Papers and Links

- [NeRF (2020)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2003.08934)
- [U2-Net (2020)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.09007)
- [NTS-Net (2018)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1809.00287)

## Production Insight

Identity constraints are crucial to prevent identity drift in restored faces. In practical delivery, I strongly recommend using a minimum loop of failure replay, metric dashboarding, and rollback plans.
